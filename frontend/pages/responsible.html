Core Principle: AI assists the evaluation. Humans validate it. Harry's rubric governs both. No student is scored without evidence. No score is final without instructor review.
6 Principles of Responsible Evaluation
1
Evidence-Based Scoring
Every score is backed by actual quotes from the student's transcript. Claude never assigns a level without finding specific behavioral evidence. Instructors can see exactly what the AI found and why it scored the way it did. No black box decisions.
2
Rubric-Governed Evaluation
All evaluations are grounded in Harry's published research-backed rubric — not AI judgment. Claude applies the rubric criteria defined by the research team. The rubric defines Level 1, 2, 3, and 4. Claude matches evidence to those definitions. Nothing is invented.
3
Confidence Transparency
Claude returns a confidence score (0–100%) with every evaluation. Low confidence scores (below 60%) are automatically flagged in red for instructor review. The AI admits when it is uncertain — instructors are never left to trust a shaky evaluation without knowing it.
4
Instructor Override
Every single AI score can be overridden by the instructor. When an override is applied, it is logged and marked clearly in the student profile. The AI is a tool to assist — not replace — professional educator judgment. Instructors always have the final word.
5
Data Privacy
Student transcript data is processed locally on your machine. No student data is stored in external databases or shared with third parties. Transcripts are sent to Claude AI for evaluation only — Anthropic's API follows strict data privacy policies. Exports are downloaded directly to your computer.
6
Consistency and Fairness
Every student is evaluated against the exact same rubric criteria, by the same AI model, with the same evaluation process. This eliminates evaluator fatigue, inconsistency, and bias that can occur in manual review of hundreds of transcripts. All students get the same standard of evaluation.
Handling Hard vs Easy Indicators
Not all indicators are equal — RubricAI adapts its approach
Easy Indicators — Countable
Example: Asks open-ended questions
Count open-ended vs closed questions
Match count to rubric threshold
High confidence, clear evidence
AI scores reliably
Hard Indicators — Behavioral
Example: Active listening · Rapport building
Detect behavioral patterns in transcript
Look for paraphrasing, callbacks, tone
Lower confidence — auto-flagged
Instructor reviews and validates
Hard indicators are always flagged for human review when confidence is low. The AI provides its best assessment with evidence, but defers to instructor judgment on qualitative behavioral indicators.
AI Disclosure Statement

AI Model Used: Claude Sonnet (Anthropic) — a large language model trained for complex reasoning and analysis tasks.

How It Evaluates: Claude reads the rubric document provided by the research team and evaluates student transcripts against the defined behavioral criteria. It does not use pre-written instructions about what to look for — the rubric itself governs all evaluations.

What It Cannot Do: Claude cannot assess tone of voice, body language, or non-verbal communication. It evaluates text-based transcripts only. Hard behavioral indicators require instructor validation.

Data Handling: Transcripts are sent to Anthropic's API for processing. No student data is permanently stored by the AI. Anthropic's data usage policies apply.

Final Authority: All AI scores are advisory. Instructors at CPS LEARN Lab retain full authority to review, modify, or override any evaluation before it is used in research or shared with partner schools.
