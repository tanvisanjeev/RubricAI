Indicator 1: Asks clear, open-ended questions
Communication Domain · Interview Communication Cluster
Live
Level Thresholds — How AI Scores
Level 1
0–1
questions
Asks 0–1 open-ended questions per 10-minute segment. Questions are vague or confusing (interviewee asks for clarification 3 or more times). Phrasing may be unclear or leading. Questions conflate multiple topics. Does not invite storytelling.
Level 2
2–3
questions
Asks 2–3 open-ended questions per 10-minute segment. Sometimes vague or confusing (clarification 2–3 times). Clear phrasing, no leading phrases. Questions sometimes conflate topics. Not strategically phrased. Does not invite storytelling.
Level 3
4–5
questions
Asks 4–5 open-ended questions per 10-minute segment (e.g., "Can you tell me about...?", "What was your experience with...?"). Clearly phrased, minimal clarification (1–2 requests). Questions focus on one topic at a time. Not yet strategically phrased for storytelling.
Level 4
6+
questions
Asks 6+ open-ended questions per 10-minute segment. Clearly phrased (less than 1 clarification request). Strategic phrasing 3–5 times: "Walk me through...", "Help me understand...", "What stood out to you about...?". Invites storytelling 2–4 times.
How Claude evaluates this indicator: Claude reads the full transcript, identifies all student questions itself — no pre-extraction. Classifies each as open-ended or closed, counts against rubric thresholds, and assigns a level with evidence quotes from the transcript.
