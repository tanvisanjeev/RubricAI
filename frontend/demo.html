About RubricAI
AI-powered rubric evaluation system for educational research

Project Overview

RubricAI evaluates student interview performance against research-backed behavioral rubrics. Developed for CPS LEARN Lab at Northeastern University to assess 300 students across 4 interviews each, processing 1,200 total transcripts with evidence-based AI evaluation.

Current Capabilities

  • Evaluates "Asks clear, open-ended questions" indicator (Levels 1-4)
  • Automated question extraction and classification from transcripts
  • Evidence-based justifications with AI confidence scoring
  • Student-specific analytics with performance tracking
  • Instructor validation workflow with override functionality

Development Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Complete - February 2026): Indicator 1 operational with test validation
  • Phase 2 (March 2026): Communication domain (10 indicators)
  • Phase 3 (April 2026): Critical Thinking domain (12 indicators)
  • Phase 4 (May 2026): Professional Agency domain (12 indicators)

Technical Architecture

  • Backend: Python FastAPI with Groq AI (Llama 3.3 70B)
  • AI Evaluation: Deductive rubric-based assessment methodology
  • Processing: Real-time transcript analysis with evidence extraction
  • Scalability: Designed to handle 1,200+ student transcripts
