Rubric Levels
Indicator 1 Level Definitions
Level 1
0–1
Asks 0–1 open-ended questions per 10-minute segment. Questions are vague or confusing. Phrasing may be unclear or leading. Does not invite storytelling.
Level 2
2–3
Asks 2–3 open-ended questions. Clear phrasing, no leading phrases. Sometimes vague. Not strategically phrased.
Level 3
4–5
Asks 4–5 open-ended questions. Clearly phrased, minimal clarification. Questions focus on one topic at a time.
Level 4
6+
Asks 6+ open-ended questions. Strategic phrasing 3–5 times: "Walk me through...", "Help me understand...". Invites storytelling 2–4 times.

Core Principle: AI assists the evaluation. Humans validate it. The rubric governs both. No student is scored without evidence. No score is final without instructor review.
6 Principles of Responsible Evaluation
1
Evidence-Based Scoring
Every score is backed by actual quotes from the student transcript. Claude never assigns a level without finding specific behavioral evidence.
2
Rubric-Governed Evaluation
All evaluations are grounded in Harry's published research-backed rubric. The rubric defines Level 1–4. Claude matches evidence to those definitions.
3
Confidence Transparency
Claude returns a confidence score with every evaluation. Low confidence (below 60%) is automatically flagged for instructor review.
4
Instructor Override
Every AI score can be overridden by the instructor. The AI assists — not replaces — professional educator judgment.
5
Data Privacy
Student data is processed locally. No data is stored externally. Exports download directly to your computer.
6
Consistency and Fairness
Every student is evaluated against the same rubric by the same AI model. Eliminates evaluator bias and fatigue.
Hard vs Easy Indicators
Easy — Countable Behaviors
Example: Asks open-ended questions
Count open-ended vs closed questions
Match count to rubric threshold
High confidence · AI scores reliably
Hard — Behavioral Patterns
Example: Active listening · Rapport
Detect behavioral patterns in transcript
Look for paraphrasing, callbacks, tone
Lower confidence · Auto-flagged for review
Hard indicators are always flagged for human review when confidence is low. The AI provides its best assessment with evidence, but defers to instructor judgment.

About RubricAI
AI-powered evaluation platform for student interview assessment
Project Overview

RubricAI evaluates student interview transcripts against a 34-indicator behavioral rubric. Built for CPS LEARN Lab at Northeastern University, it provides consistent, evidence-based scoring — replacing manual, subjective review with scalable AI-assisted assessment that instructors validate and control.

The tool evaluates student communication, critical thinking, and professional agency skills as demonstrated in interview simulations. All evaluations are grounded in research-backed rubric criteria.
